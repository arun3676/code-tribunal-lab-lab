The question frontier reviewers can't ask

Did the AI build what you actually asked for?

Every frontier code reviewer reads the diff and asks "is this correct?" None of them can ask "is this the change that was requested?" — because the intent lives outside the repo. Tribunal puts a change on trial for faithfulness: a panel of agents reconciles what was asked, what was done, and the gap between them.

VERDICT: DOES NOT CONFORM

01 · The structural gap

The intent ceiling

A structural analyzer can read what code does; it has no access to what the code was supposed to do. That boundary now has a name in the 2026 literature — and the tool to cross it is openly described as not existing in production yet. It can't be crossed by one model reading harder. It's crossed by giving different agents the intent the reviewer never sees.

02 · Who needs this every day

The 600-line "done" problem
The setup

You ask an agent for X

Cursor, Claude Code, Codex. One sentence in.

The output

A 600-line diff that says "done"

Past ~400 lines, human review degrades into rubber-stamping. You can't hold it in your head.

The doubt

Did it really do X?

The hardest bug is the gap between what the spec said and what the model thought it meant. Confident, wrong, and invisible.

Same problem, enterprise scale: the ledger Tribunal produces is a traceability record for SOC 2 / EU AI Act audits — proof of which intent governed which change.

Why GHOST matters

Negative space

Frontier tools review what's present. GHOST is the only one looking for what's absent relative to intent.

Why DRIFT matters

The sneak-in

The "what did the AI quietly add?" check. Unrequested refactors, silent behavior changes, surprise dependencies.

04 · Why it has to be Band

The verdict is impossible without coordination

No single agent holds intent and code and tests and policy. So they meet in a Band room: @mention-directed messages for the argument, a separate Events stream for evidence, and live recruitment when a specialist is needed. That last part — pulling WARDEN in mid-trial — is the Band primitive most teams will never touch.

07 · Against the judging rubric

One concept, four boxes ticked

Application of technology

The verdict can't be produced by one model — it's a reconciliation across agents, with on-demand recruitment, structured Events, and @mention routing. Band is the load-bearing layer.

Business value

Crosses the intent ceiling: the daily "did the AI do what I asked?" trust gap, and the enterprise audit-trail requirement, in one artifact.

Originality

Not PR review. Intent-conformance adjudication with omission + scope-drift detection — beyond Codeband (correctness) and beyond opaque spec-generators.

Presentation

A change on trial: personas summoned live, evidence chips, a verdict stamp, a ledger. Legible in five minutes of video.
